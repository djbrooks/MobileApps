Lesson_09 Hands-On: 12 Column Grid

Throughout the Responsive Design course, we have been introduced to Responsive Design and how it can be leverage to make our applications function better across multiple platforms. A major key in this capability is the ability to use grid systems. With a grid, we can have our elements organized and displayed proportionally across various devices. In this Hands-On Project, we will be creating our very own grid systems that we can use on future projects.

Requirements:

  • 12 Column Grid
  • A Side Navbar that uses the left-most two columns
  • A header to use the remaining 10 columns.
  • 3 separate columns of text, each take up 4 columns in our grid.
  • Two images, each position to take up six columns.
  • A footer to use the full length of 12 columns.

Description:

Following the elements you have learned in the previous lessons, create a custom grid using a fluid 12 column grid structure. We will then use that grid to place some items on our page. This will allow us to practice placing items of various sizes on our web page.

Tip! When designing your Robot Store, have a theme in mind. This can include everything from a color scheme to the placement of items on the page. For inspiration view your favorite online stores.

Grading:

  • Meets all Requirements: 50% based on meeting requirements.
  • Timely Submission: 25% based on complete solution delivered on time.
  • Style: 25% based on legible and well-designed code.

Be sure to save your solution, and be prepared to share it with your Instructor or Mentor during your next class, or check-in.

Tip! Your Instructor or Mentor may test your program with additional inputs, so be sure to test it thoroughly with different scenarios.
